Diffeomorphisms: Gauge transformations involving a ghost field $c^\mu$ that acts as an infinitesimal Lie derivative on $\varphi$, $V^\nu$, and $S$.

Ghost Closure: $Qc^\mu = c^\nu \partial_\nu c^\mu$ ensures the Lie algebra structure of the vector fields.

Antifield Sector: Antifields ($\varphi^+$, $V^{+\mu}$, $S^+$, $c_\mu^+$) encode the equations of motion and serve as sources for BRST variations, collecting Noether currents.

BV Differential ($Q$): A nilpotent differential ($Q^2=0$) that formalizes the gauge symmetries and leads to a dg-manifold structure on field space. Its cohomology captures gauge-invariant observables.

C. Obstruction Theory
In derived geometry, obstruction theory determines when deformations of mathematical objects (like field configurations) can be extended.

Cotangent Complex: Provides tangent space (deformations) in Ext$^0$ and obstruction space (obstructions) in Ext$^1$.

Derived Infinitesimal Lifting Property: A map can be lifted if an obstruction class vanishes, and the space of lifts forms a torsor.

Maurer-Cartan Elements: Perturbations of RSVP's vacuum can be described as Maurer-Cartan elements in a dg-Lie algebra associated with the cotangent complex, with obstruction theory emerging from the Lie bracket.

Answer Key
The three primary fields are the scalar field ($\varphi$ or $\phi$) representing local plenum density, the vector field ($V^\mu$) representing negentropic vector flux, and the scalar entropy field ($S$) governing local state-space degeneracy. These fields are non-trivially coupled to describe the universe's evolution.

In the 1D Lattice RSVP model, the scalar field ($\phi$) is advected by the vector field ($V$), similar to dye in a stream. Conversely, the vector field ($V$) responds to entropy gradients but exhibits resistance to high values of the scalar field ($\phi$).

Entropy descent in RSVP dynamics is the principle that field evolution occurs as a gradient flow of entropy. It is mathematically expressed as $\frac{d}{dt} \Phi(x,t) = -\frac{\delta \mathcal{S}}{\delta \Phi}$, where $\mathcal{S}[\Phi]$ is a thermodynamic action functional, indicating that fields evolve towards states of lower free energy or entropy.

The BV formalism is a quantization framework used to handle gauge symmetries in RSVP and DLSSM, introducing ghosts and antifields for consistency. The condition $Q^2=0$ signifies the nilpotency of the BV differential, which is essential for ensuring that the gauge symmetries are well-defined and the cohomology captures true physical observables.

Ethical-Causal Consistency is a critical condition for the consistency of the DLSSM action, expressed as $\int \frac{\delta \epsilon}{\delta \phi} \frac{\delta \mathcal{L}{\text{rewrite}}}{\delta \phi^\dagger} = 0$. It relates the ethical boundary term ($\epsilon$) to the symbolic rewriting term ($\mathcal{L}{\text{rewrite}}$), suggesting a deep connection between ethical considerations and the fundamental dynamics of the system.

Derived stacks are advanced geometric objects that model fuzzy or quantum spaces, encoding RSVP field configurations and spacetime. The cotangent complex ($\mathbb{L}_{\mathcal{X}}$) is used to study the infinitesimal deformation theory of these stacks, revealing how fields perturb and allowing for a detailed analysis of their fluctuations and constraints.

L-systems (Lindenmayer systems) are used to model recursive self-assembly and growth within the DLSSM framework. They are reinterpreted as "rewriting bundles as sheaves," providing a formal mechanism for cosmic rewriting through symbolic path integrals within the action functional.

The TARTAN framework uses persistent cohomology to encode the history of recursive tiling spaces, tracking their evolution via Eilenberg-MacLane stacks. It defines a Wasserstein metric on persistence diagrams to topologize noise, allowing for semantically precise comparison of evolved plenum states and handling historical data.

In the 1D Lattice RSVP model, low entropy leads to a smooth vector field ($V_i$) and the formation of stable waves in the scalar field ($\phi$). Conversely, high entropy results in a chaotic vector field ($V_i$) and the fragmentation of the scalar field ($\phi$).

The ethical boundary term $\epsilon(\Phi)$ in the DLSSM action is intended to incorporate value alignment into the system's dynamics. However, a critique suggests it might be a "moral mirage" if its consistency is not rigorously guaranteed, particularly without the presence of a derived symplectic stack to ensure the validity of the kinetic term.

Glossary of Key Terms
1D Lattice RSVP Model: A simplified numerical simulation model for RSVP, used to study the behavior of scalar ($\phi$) and vector ($V$) fields under varying entropy conditions with specific boundary conditions.

Action Functional ($S$): A mathematical expression (typically an integral of a Lagrangian density) whose variation with respect to fields yields the equations of motion for a physical system.

Antifields: Auxiliary fields introduced in the BV formalism, paired with original fields and ghosts, that serve as sources for BRST variations and encode equations of motion.

BV Formalism (Batalin-Vilkovisky Formalism): A mathematical framework for quantizing gauge theories, using an extended phase space (including ghosts and antifields) and a nilpotent differential ($Q$) to handle gauge symmetries.

Classifying $\infty$-stack ($BG = pt//G$): In derived geometry, a generalization of a classifying space that parameterizes principal G-bundles, encoding higher homotopical information.

Cotangent Complex ($\mathbb{L}_{\mathcal{X}}$): A fundamental object in derived algebraic geometry that encodes the infinitesimal deformation theory of a derived stack or scheme, indicating tangent and obstruction spaces.

Cosmic Cycles: A concept within RSVP describing repetitive phases of cosmic evolution, potentially involving "expyrosis" or a "cosmic reset" from quantum fluctuations.

Derived Affine Scheme ($\text{Spec}(A)$): A foundational object in derived algebraic geometry, generalizing classical affine schemes by allowing the underlying ring $A$ to be a simplicial commutative ring, encoding homotopical information.

Derived Étale Descent: A condition satisfied by derived stacks, ensuring that their local data can be consistently glued together to form global objects, accounting for homotopical information.

Derived Stack ($\mathcal{X}$): A generalization of a classical stack or manifold that incorporates homotopical information, mapping simplicial commutative rings to $\infty$-groupoids (spaces). They are used to model "fuzzy" or "quantum" geometric objects.

DG-Lie Algebra ($\mathfrak{g}$): A differential graded Lie algebra, a mathematical structure used in deformation theory to encode the tangent complex and obstruction theory of moduli spaces.

Diffeomorphism Symmetry: A type of gauge symmetry associated with the invariance of a theory under coordinate transformations of spacetime. In RSVP, this is handled by ghosts and antifields in the BV formalism.

DLSSM (Derived L-System Sigma Models): A theoretical framework that reinterprets RSVP phenomena as emergent properties using derived field theories, L-systems, and ethical considerations.

Entropy Descent: A dynamical principle in RSVP stating that the system evolves towards states of lower free energy or entropy, formalized as a gradient flow.

Entropy Field ($S$): A scalar field in RSVP that quantifies the local state-space degeneracy of the plenum, with gravity reinterpreted as its gradient.

Ethical-Causal Consistency: A condition within the DLSSM framework ($\int \frac{\delta \epsilon}{\delta \phi} \frac{\delta \mathcal{L}_{\text{rewrite}}}{\delta \phi^\dagger} = 0$) that links the ethical boundary term to the rewriting dynamics, ensuring the consistency of the BV bracket.

Ethical Potential ($\epsilon(\Phi)$): A term in the DLSSM action functional intended to represent ethical considerations or "value alignment," potentially acting as a Lagrangian derived substack restricting field trajectories.

Ghosts: Auxiliary fields introduced in gauge theories (like in the BV formalism) that carry a specific "ghost number" and help cancel unphysical degrees of freedom arising from gauge redundancy.

Gray-coded L-words: Specific sequences generated by L-systems that model L-system tilings, used as discrete local models of the thermodynamic plenum in the DLSSM framework.

Homological Vector Field ($Q$): A degree-1 vector field on a dg-manifold (or field space) whose square is zero ($Q^2=0$), defining the BV differential and encoding gauge symmetries.

Homotopy Colimit ($\mathrm{hocolim}$): A construction in homotopy theory that generalizes the concept of a colimit (e.g., union) to "homotopy-coherent" settings, used in RSVP to describe cosmic reset.

L-systems (Lindenmayer Systems): Formal grammar systems used to model the growth and self-assembly of biological organisms; in DLSSM, they model recursive self-assembly and cosmic rewriting.

Lagrangian Derived Substack: A concept in derived symplectic geometry where a substack inherits a Lagrangian structure, often used to restrict field trajectories to physically admissible ones, as with the ethical term $\epsilon$.

Maurer-Cartan Elements: Elements in a dg-Lie algebra that satisfy the Maurer-Cartan equation, corresponding to "flat connections" or solutions to deformation problems.

Obstruction Theory: A mathematical theory, often using the cotangent complex, that identifies conditions under which a local deformation or a map can be extended globally or lifted to a higher structure.

Persistent Cohomology: A tool from topological data analysis that studies the evolution of topological features (e.g., holes, connected components) in a filtered space, used in TARTAN to track tiling evolution.

Plenum Geometry: The geometric description of the "plenum," a fundamental, undifferentiated substrate of reality in RSVP, often modeled using tiling stacks or L-systems.

Quasi-coherent Sheaves ($\mathcal{QCoh}$): Sheaves whose local sections are modules over the local rings, fundamental in algebraic geometry for studying geometric objects through their associated algebraic structures.

Recursive Self-assembly: A process where components assemble themselves into larger structures following recursive rules, modeled by L-systems in RSVP.

Rewriting Bundles as Sheaves: The interpretation of L-system rewriting rules as structures within sheaf theory, providing a framework for how symbolic evolution impacts the plenum.

RSVP (Relaxation-driven Smoothing and Variational Plenum): A theoretical model positing a plenum undergoing internal differentiation, with gravity reinterpreted as an entropy gradient and cosmic evolution as a smoothing process.

Scalar Field ($\varphi$ or $\phi$): A field that assigns a scalar value to each point in spacetime; in RSVP, it represents local plenum density or potential, advected by the vector flow.

Shifted Symplectic Form: A generalization of a symplectic form in derived geometry, allowing the "degree" of the form to be shifted. A $(-1)$-shifted symplectic form is common in BV formalism and produces a BV antibracket.

Simplicial Commutative Rings ($\mathbf{sCRing}$): Algebraic structures that encode homotopical information in rings, forming the domain category for derived stacks.

TARTAN Framework: A framework that addresses blind spots in RSVP, focusing on typed causality, noise metric structure via persistence diagrams, and the formalization of ethical terms.

Tiling Stacks ($P_{\text{tile}}$): Derived stacks whose points represent geometric tilings, specifically Gray-coded L-system tilings, which serve as discrete local models of the thermodynamic plenum.

Typed Causality: A concept in TARTAN employing linear dependent types to ensure well-founded recursion and prevent causal loops, requiring termination witnesses for operations.

Vector Field ($V^\mu$): A field that assigns a vector to each point in spacetime; in RSVP, it represents directed constraint relaxation or negentropic flux, responding to entropy gradients.

Wasserstein Metric ($d_\mathcal{W}$): A metric used to measure the distance between probability distributions or persistence diagrams, employed in TARTAN to topologize noise and compare plenum states.
